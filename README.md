# Cybersecurity-Ready-Agentic-AI-for-SOC-Automation
This paper presents an agentic artificial intelligence framework for Security Operations Center (SOC) automation, addressing the limitations of signature-based intrusion detection under large-scale, multi-vector cyber threats. Existing SOC systems suffer from alert fatigue, lack of contextual reasoning, and the absence of governed automation with explicit safety boundaries. A four-agent architecture is proposed, comprising Log Analysis, Threat Correlation, Threat Enrichment, and Threat Escalation agents, forming an end-to-end governed pipeline not addressed by prior work. The framework employs XGBoost with Synthetic Minority Oversampling Technique as the primary detector, benchmarked against Random Forest and Isolation Forest, and introduces a bounded-autonomy escalation policy based on confidence and novelty thresholds that routes uncertain or high-severity cases to human analysts. Experiments on the CIC IDS-2017 dataset (500,000 records) show that XGBoost achieves 98.87% accuracy, 0.9617 F1-score, and 318.688ms inference latency. The agentic pipeline produces 17,348 correlated incidents, routing 4,265 autonomously and escalating 13,083 to human review at 93.81% incident precision. Robustness analysis reveals significant sensitivity to feature perturbation, underscoring the need for adversarial hardening. SHapley Additive exPlanations based interpretability identifies Flow Duration, Flow Packets per second, and Total Forward Packets as dominant escalation drivers. The results validate that controlled-autonomy SOC operation achieves high detection performance while preserving human oversight and operational safety.
